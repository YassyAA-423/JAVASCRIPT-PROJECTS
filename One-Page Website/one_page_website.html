Horror

“We like novelty, something that departs from our everyday experience,” he said. “Some of the attraction of being scared comes from the deviation of having a new experience that we know is safe.” by Irving Biederman

What is horror?... And why does a good chunk of humanity enjoy it? That has been asked and generally studied throughout the years….

... But we are actually not going to answer that here… Instead, we are here to show a little love to some not-so-well-known works of horror.

Different Forms of Horror

Horror can come in many forms. Honestly, it's amazing just how creative people can be when it comes to this. But my goal for this is to show examples of media that you may not have heard much about (and if you do, that's great!). I'll still mention a few big names here and there, but I won't be using them as the actual example.

Board Games

You probably weren't expecting to see board games involved in this genre, but there are surprisingly quite a few. And no, I am not talking about the dreaded "Perfection Game" by Hasbro. That ticking still spikes up my anxiety… In this case, I'm talking about horror board games that use tension in a more creative way.

Black Mold

Black mold was created by Terrible Games. Between 2 - 5 people can play. In this board game, you play as prisoners who have been abandoned during a fungal infection. Your goal is to escape this underground prison, but during your turn, you have to hold your breath (or use the 60-second sand timer) to stumble your way through this labyrinth. But be careful… the longer you stay, the higher the chance you get infected… becoming one of the monsters lurking in this prison.(Also highly recommend "The Night cage")

Video Games

An obvious form of horror is video games, it can be subtle psychological horror games like "Night in the Woods" to Survival Horror games like "Resident Evil". These are wonderful examples of horror because you have to be involved! Your decisions influence whether you will get a good or bad ending.

Who's Lila

Who's Lila is a point-and-click reverse detective game, where you can control the character's expression. In this game, you mostly play a character called William. Something strange is happening around you, and you are a suspect in Tanya's Disappearance. Throughout the gameplay, your choices are based on the character's expression. You can't help but feel unsettled as you play through.. Finding clues to what happened… who and how Lila is connected in all this.

Story-Telling

The most well-known form of horror is, of course, storytelling—originally passed down by word of mouth. This form has evolved and been better preserved through writing and recording. Books like "Scary Stories to Tell in the Dark" by Alvin Schwartz, websites such as "Creepypasta.com", and podcasts like "Wake of Corrosion" all continue this long tradition of sharing frightening tales.

Radio Silence

"Radio Silence" by Bencbarlett, read by MrCreepyPasta, is a short 5-minute story. Though it is short, it gives a what-if answer to the question "What if we're not alone?". By the time it's done, you're left feeling unsettled...

Scary movies

If anything, Horror films in general a very popular way to consume this genre. From the grief-horror like "The Babadook" directed by Jennifer Kent to Classics like "Psycho" directed by Alfred Hitchcock. For this one, we'll focus on Short films. There are tons of wonderful horror short films that explore and take more risks than big-screen films.

Safe Haven

Now I do have a few favorite short horror films. I narrowed it down to this one for one reason... The ending. Not to spoil too much, but “Safe Haven” by Martin Stalker is about a family who is trying to survive a post-apocalyptic climate disaster. It's roughly 16 minutes, but if you want a "sit-at-the-edge-of-your-seat" short film, I highly recommend this one. (I also highly recommend "All You Can Carry" by Andrew Rose)

Basically, what I am saying is that humans are incredibly creative. While I have talked about a few different forms of horror, there are still many I haven't gone through… So by all means, explore and be curious. And remember that Horror doesn't mean you have to go through it alone. It's always much more fun… and comforting... to have friends or loved ones to experience them with you.
